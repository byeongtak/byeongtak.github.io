About Me
Over the past seven years, I have been working as ML researcher in two healthcare start-ups. As an industrial researcher, I have had the opportunity to develop various AI solutions for real-world healthcare applications, focusing on ECG and EHR data, while addressing ML challenges such as domain generalization and noisy label. Additionally, I have experience in developing AutoML system for medical data.

I believe AI can liberate humanity from disease, and I am committed to contributing towards this. Particularly, I am interested in applying ML to bioinformatics, including genomics and structural biology, while tackling technical challenges in ML that emerge during in this process. The detail of my research experience and interest are described in Research Statement.

Keywords: AI in Healthcare, Domain generalization, Noisy label, AutoML system, Bioinformatics
Projects
At Medial AI Co Ltd, I developed cardiovascular disease screening products (AITIA Series) that were conferred as an innovative medical device from the Korean FDA and won the UNIST-UCLA Digital Healthcare Challenge in 2023. They are currently used in many clinics and hospitals across Korea.

Publications
My publications are about (1) developing and evaluating AI models in clinical practice or (2) tackling ML challenges in applying AI to medical data.

Developing and evaluating AI models in clinical practice: As an industrial researcher, my work had centered on the question: "How can AI improve medical practice?" Thus, I developed models for sepsis prediction using EHR, cardiovascular disease diagnosis with ECG, and blood pressure estimation using ECG and PPG.

Tackling ML challenges in medical data: However, there were several challenges in this process, such as performance drops when testing on data with demographics different from the training dataset. Data quality posed another problems, including missing values in EHR, noise in ECG, and incorrect labels. Addressing these challenges were important for the use of AI-based model used in medical practice, which is now the focus of my work.
