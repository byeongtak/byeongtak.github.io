About Me
I worked as a machine learning researcher in the medical domain over seven years, where I developed a variety of ML-based tools for diagnosis and screening. However, I realized that many of the diseases we can now detect with these tool still lack definitive treatements. This led me to develop an interest in biology, as it offers the chance to uncover the fundamental causes of disease and contribute to the development of new therapeutics. To take this next step, I am currently studying computational biology at Purdue University.
Research Interest
Biology & Medicine: Proteomics, Drug Discovery, Single-cell and spatial transcriptomics, Electrocardiogram, Electronic hospital records
Machine Learning: Automated machine learning, Domain generalization, Generative Machine Learning, Noisy Label, Structual inductive biases of neural networks
